Employment Status Definitions

The Vernon Company maintains standard definitions of employment status and classifies employees for purposes for personnel administration and related payroll transactions according to the following definitions:

Exempt: Employees whose positions meet specific tests established by the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) and state law and who are exempt from overtime pay requirements.

Nonexempt: Employees whose positions do not meet Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) exemption tests and who are paid overtime, as required by federal and state laws.

Salaried: Employees who are typically hired as an exempt employee. This may be a supervisor, manager, professional level (or above) position.

Administrative: Employees who are typically hired as a nonexempt employee. This may be in a clerical, coordinator, specialist, lead, supervisor (or similar job) in an administrative type position who reports to a manager level (or above) position.

Full-time: Administrative (nonexempt) employees normally scheduled to work thirty (30) hours or more per week, and all salaried (exempt) employees. They are eligible for full-range benefits and vacation/holiday pay.

Part-time: Administrative (nonexempt) employees normally scheduled to work less than thirty (30) hours per week. Regular Part-Time employees are eligible for pro-rated vacation/holiday pay based on their hours worked.

Regular: Employees who are hired as either full-time or part-time (and not on a temporary-basis).

Temporary: Employees who are hired on a temporary-basis, for a per-established period (with an end date), usually during peak workloads. They may work full-time or part-time. They are not eligible for full-range benefits or vacation/holiday pay.

Equal Employment Opportunity

Purpose: To provide equal employment opportunities to all employees and applicants.

Policy: The Vernon Company is fully committed to providing equal employment opportunities for all employees and applicants. It is our policy to recruit, hire, train and promote into all job levels, any employee or applicant for employment regardless of race, color, sex, national origin, religion, age, disability, status as a disabled or Vietnam-era veteran, or any other characteristic protected by law. The Company also prohibits harassment on these bases. All such decisions are based on individual merit, qualifications and competence as they relate to the particular position.

Job Posts & Recruiting

Purpose: The Vernon Company posts job opening utilizing a variety of resources, including Company bulletin boards, intranet (internal website), internet (external website), recruiters/agencies, and/or other recruiting methods.

Policy: Internal job postings are placed on Company bulletin boards for approximately three (3) days. In addition, Company positions may be posted on The Vernon Company’s intranet (internal website).

Employees are eligible to bid on a posted job if they have been in their current position for at least one (1) year. We ask that employees inform their manager/supervisor when and if they are asked to be interviewed for an internal position. Employees who have been employed by the Company for less than one (1) year are eligible to bid on another job position only if their current manager/supervisor approves, prior to applying. Positions may also be posted externally on The Vernon Company’s internet (external website), and/or we may use alternative recruiting methods. Resumes are required for all externally posted positions. We prefer that resumes be e-mailed, faxed and/or mailed to Human Resources, rather than individually handling walk-ins. Applicants need to apply for a specific position. (Applications are required for all production position openings, and will be accepted when a specific position is open.) Work Force is an additional source for recruiting externally. Other forms of recruiting may be utilized on an as-needed basis.

Employee Referrals

Purpose: To receive referrals from House Office employees for potential, qualified and suitable new hires. We will reward current employees who refer individuals who stay with The Vernon Company for six (6) consecutive months or longer.

Policy: For an employee referral to be valid, a Referral Form must be completed and returned to Human Resources prior to the candidate interviewing. Human Resources will attach this form to the candidate’s employment application. You will receive a bonus after the new employee who you referred has been employed with The Vernon Company for six (6) consecutive months, and all rules were followed. To receive the bonus, you must be an active employee at the time the new employee reaches the six (6) month mark. You will receive the bonus in a normal paycheck, not in a separate check. There is no limit to the number of qualified people you refer to The Vernon Company. For a current list of position openings and bonus amount details, contact Human Resources. Referral Forms can be obtained in Human Resources. In addition to this Home Office Referral Program, you can participate in the Sales Referral Program to receive bonus dollars. See Sales or Human Resources for further details on the Sales Referral Program.

Orientation Period

Purpose: To complete the new employee process by providing a formal and planned period by which the new employee and The Vernon Company may evaluate employment suitability in terms of knowledge, skills, ability, performance and interest.

Policy: As a standard, new full-time and/or part-time employees will be in an ‘orientation period’ for the first sixty (60) days of employment. Management may choose to extend the period at the time of hire or after, if needed. A review will be completed by the manager/supervisor after the orientation period to inform the employee of his/her performance.

Completion of an orientation period, or conferral of regular status, does not change an employee’s status as an employee-at-will.

Hybrid Work

Purpose: The Vernon Company (the “Company”) believes that our business practices are best served when employees are onsite.   The Company also recognizes that alternative work arrangements such as hybrid arrangements may provide advantages for both the Company and its employees, including, but not limited to:  increased productivity and performance, enhanced employee recruitment and retention, cost reduction, and greater work-life balance.  In accordance with the guidelines set forth in this Hybrid Work Arrangement Policy, the purpose of this policy is to describe the hybrid work arrangement that may be available to certain employees in appropriate circumstances.

Hybrid Work Arrangement Policy Statement: This policy addresses hybrid work arrangements.  A hybrid work arrangement is appropriate for some employees and some jobs.  No employee is entitled to or guaranteed the opportunity to hybrid work.  Certain categories of positions or departments are ineligible for hybrid work arrangements. The Company reserves the right to approve or deny a hybrid work arrangement request, and to modify or revoke such arrangement at its sole discretion.

The current hybrid policy is to work at least two days in the office per week, which must be agreed upon with the manager.

Eligibility: Current employees in good standing requesting a hybrid/remote work arrangement should be employed by the Company for a minimum of 12 months of continuous, regular employment. Exceptions to this rule may be made at the discretion of the Manager and Human Resources.

Eligible Positions for Hybrid/Remote Work: When evaluating whether the duties of a particular position may be performed hybrid/remotely, the supervisor should consider the following:

  • Nature of the work and responsibilities to be performed are conducive to a hybrid/remote work location without causing disruption to performance or service;
  • The needs of the department to advance its core mission and the needs of the department to fulfill its role in that mission: including frequency of meetings and department goals and projects;
  • Other collaborating departments’ needs and schedules;
  • Whether or not daily face-to-face in-person interaction is required of the position;
  • The job does not require onsite employee supervision.
  • An excellent level of service can be maintained without hardship on collaborating departments and co-workers.
  • Identification of a backup to monitor the hybrid/remote worker in the supervisor’s absence.
  • The performance of the employee.

General Expectations and Conditions: Employee job duties and responsibilities will not change due to a hybrid work arrangement.  Professionalism in terms of job responsibilities, work output, and service delivery will continue to follow the standards set by the Company.  The employee must be able to maintain performance, productivity, and quality standards.  Employees must be fully accessible during agreed upon work hours. 

Managers may require employees working a hybrid work arrangement to report to the central workplace as needed for work-related meetings or other events, office coverage, or as needed to discuss work progress or other work-related issues.  Employees are expected to perform their work during core or agreed hybrid work hours and should be available through email and phone, just as if they were in the office. Reasonable amounts of time for breaks and meal breaks are permitted. In the event that an employee loses their internet coverage, and it continues for one hour, the employee is expected to report to the Company for the remainder of their scheduled workday. Employees must notify their supervisor/manager immediately if this situation arises. If it is found an employee is not performing work during the core or agreed hybrid work hours, the hybrid work arrangement may be terminated, and the employee may be subject to discipline.

Employees working a hybrid work schedule must comply with all Company policies, procedures, and guidelines and all applicable local, state, and federal laws.  Failure to comply with Company policies and procedures may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

All non-exempt employees must record all hours worked in accordance with the Company’s time reporting policies and procedures and the Fair Labor Standards Act.  Non-exempt employees must report their work hours accurately and timely and obtain prior approval before working overtime.

Liability: Workers’ compensation coverage is limited to designated work areas in employees’ homes or alternate work locations. Employees agree to practice the same safety habits they would use in the Company and to maintain safe conditions in their alternate work locations. Employees must follow normal procedures for reporting illness or injury.

Modification or Termination of Alternative Work Arrangement: A supervisor or Manager, in consultation with their Vice President and Human Resources, may modify or terminate the Hybrid Work Agreement at any time for performance concerns, changing operational needs, if the Agreement is no longer in the best interests of the Company, or for any other legitimate, non-discriminatory business reason.

Travel/Expenses: All travel and expenses must adhere to the Business Expense Policy.  Travel time between the central workplace and alternate work location for hybrid workers is generally not paid unless it is part of the Employee’s normally scheduled workday.  Travel expenses between the alternate work location and the central workplace are not reimbursed.

Layoffs, Transfers & Reclassification

Purpose: To accommodate changing levels of work (or lack of work) in various departments.

Policy: If the Company determines that the present workload is insufficient to maintain current levels of employment, employees may be laid-off, transferred or reclassified to another position. This may occur to reduce employees in one area and increase employees in another, due to workload conditions.

If you are transferred to another position, your manager/supervisor and Human Resources will determine whether your job classification and pay rate will stay the same or change.

Separations

Purpose: To ensure timely, accurate processing of employee records and benefits as well as provide the company with adequate time to select an employee replacement, when and if applicable.

Policy: In the even of your separation from employment with The Vernon Company, your separation date shall be the last day you work. A ‘separation’ includes a voluntary termination/resignation, involuntary termination or retirement.

Your final check will include payment for any Paid Time Off (PTO) available based on your hire date. Employees need to work on the day of their separation. Paid Time Off cannot extend a separation or benefits end date.

For resignations and retirements, a minimum of two (2) weeks written notice is required for employees. However, four (4) weeks written notice is requested for salaried (exempt) employees. Written notice must be provided to the manager/supervisor and Human Resources by the employee. An exit interview will be scheduled by Human Resources prior to the separation date.

Performance Appraisals (Reviews)

Purpose: To provide a formal process by which the job performance of each employee is appraised (reviewed) for purposes of discussing and measuring effectiveness of work, communication and development.

Policy: The annual employee performance appraisal (review) process will be managed to accomplish the following objectives:

  • To enhance individual employee performance as it relates to Company objectives.
  • To summarize both formal and informal performance discussions held between managers/supervisors and employees throughout the review period.
  • To document performance standards that are currently being met or exceeded, and areas which require improvement.
  • To establish performance goals and plans for individual training and/or development.

Each manager/supervisor is responsible for setting and communicating clear performance standards for his/her employees at the beginning of, and throughout the review period. Each manager/supervisor is also responsible for observing and discussing with his/her employees, both positive and/or negative aspects of their performance throughout the review period. New hires will receive a sixty (60) day (or longer) review by their manager/supervisor. In addition, all employees will receive an annual performance appraisal (review) in the March or April time frame. Any compensation rate adjustments will typically be effective sometime during the month of April.

Personnel Records

Purpose: To establish standards by which information contained in personnel records will be managed.

Policy: Information within The Vernon Company files or records relating to your personnel information is considered part of the Company’s records. The Vernon Company reserves the right to utilize that information for business purposes and will provide any such required information to be disclosed by law, court order, or upon reasonable request by law enforcement personnel.

Changes of address, telephone number and/or family status (births, deaths, dependent changes, marriage, divorce, legal separation, etc.) must be reported immediately to Human Resources, as an employee’s income tax status, group insurance and other factors may be affected by these changes.

Employees may request access to their files for review and copying. Any copies made will be at the expense of the employee, and shall not exceed $5.00. The review of a file has to be scheduled during normal working hours, Monday – Friday, 7:25am – 4:25pm. The scheduled time has to be convenient for the employee and Human Resources, both parties which shall be present during the review of the file.

Requests for information from employee files received from other departments and inquiries from outside The Vernon Company, including requests for references on former employees, need to be directed to Human Resources. Management and other employees are prohibited from providing employment references on former employees or current employees without approval from Human Resources.
