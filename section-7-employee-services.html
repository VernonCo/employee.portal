Education Assistance (Tuition Reimbursement)

Purpose: To provide financial assistance to active full-time and part-time employees who attend educational, job-related courses.

Policy: For job-related, professional instruction, the Company will provide financial assistance to pay tuition. Please note, books and mileage are not covered by the Company when an employee asks to attend an eligible course.

To obtain educational assistance, complete the necessary forms from Human Resources, which will include the course number, course name, an outline of the course, educational facility, course cost, your division head’s approval, and other related information. Human Resources and management will determine the percent of reimbursement to be paid by the Company. The tuition reimbursement forms must be completed and approved prior to the start of the course.

The Company will reimburse the employee the approved tuition mount at the successful completion of the course if the following criteria have been met:

  • Provide proof of successful completion (a grade of ‘C’ or above, or ‘Passing’) to Human Resources
  • Course must be taken at an accredited college or technical school
  • Limited one three-hour course or equivalent per semester; maximum three courses per year
  • If employment is terminated, voluntary or otherwise, prior to one year of service after tuition reimbursement has been paid out, the employee will be responsible for reimbursing The Vernon Company for all tuition that has been paid out to the employee. This is based on a twelve-month rolling period. This policy will use the completion date for the course(s) as the start of the twelve-month rolling period.

College Scholarships

Purpose: To offer scholarships each year for qualified children of Vernon Company employees.

Policy: The Vernon Company offers a maximum of three (3), renewable $1,000 scholarships each year to qualified children of Vernon Company employees. To be eligible, the applicant must be a senior in a n accredited high school, and the applicant’s parent must be an active employee of The Vernon Company.

Applications will be distributed in the spring each year, and must be returned in September. Each applicant will receive a packet of materials containing instructions and materials which should be completed and returned to Human Resources. Applications will be submitted to the Selection Committee for their recommendations. Deadlines are typically in February. It is the responsibility of the employee and applicant (student) to meet necessary deadlines which have been communicated. The Selection Committee, which is made up of professional educators, will select new recipients and review applications each year in March. Decisions are made based on various factors including academics, strength/challenge/consistent performance of curriculum as well as how many academic subjects are chosen by the student. Other factors include, but are not limited to GPA, SAT/ACT scores, school activities, community involvement, whether the student is working while attending school, conduct of the student (interpersonal skills, positive contributions, leadership skills, reliability, timeliness and whether they take imitative to do more without being asked).

Scholarships are for the payment of tuition. Each scholarship recipient will be asked to submit the address of their institution’s tuition payment office (Registrars Office). Each semester, scholarship checks will be mailed directly to the scholarship recipients.

Other various scholarships may be offered throughout the year. Details will be communicated by Human Resources as they become available.

Credit Union Service

Purpose: The Vernon Credit Union has been established for employees to save money or borrow at low interest rates.

Service: The Credit Union is typically open, 12:00 noon to 1:00pm, Mondays and Wednesdays for employee transactions. If you are interested in joining the Credit Union, contact Human Resources for necessary payroll deduction forms. When the appropriate forms have been completed, the Payroll Department will start your payroll deductions. Members who work in the Credit Union, do so on their own time, not Company time. The Credit Union is completely independent from the Company, and accordingly, has sole responsibility for assuring accountability for money collected and conducting itself in accordance with the current rules governing credit unions.

UPDATE: Effective August 1st, 2011, the Vernon Credit Union closed and merged with Community Choice Credit Union.

Bulletin Boards, Communication Station & Postings

Purpose: To provide an official channel of communication to employees.

Policy: Important information will be displayed permanently on bulletin boards located at strategic points throughout the office and production facility. If an employee posts information on Company bulletin boards, it is his/her responsibility to remove the posting in a timely manner. We ask that employees do not post or lay advertisements in the restrooms or other areas. However, you may utilize the bulletin boards and table by office bulletin board. Information needs to be of the following types:

  • Legally required posters and notices
  • Safety Rules, emergency information and related information
  • Management information
  • Human Resources information, including job postings
  • Social and recreational events
  • Employee announcements or ads appropriate for posting (See Human Resources for questions)

Safety Committee

Purpose: To provide safety awareness to employees, help eliminate hazards, and prevent work-related injuries and illnesses.

Service: The Vernon Company believes in quality, efficiency, and success in every phase of our operation. We attribute these achievements to the hard work and dedication of our employees. We are committed to reducing work-related injuries and illnesses because our most valuable resource is you – the employee. We abide by the premise that job safety and health is a manageable function, and we expect all levels of employees to take necessary steps to prevent injuries, illnesses, as well as to take care of Company property and prevent equipment damage. We ask that every employee strive to meet our goal of accident reduction by following safe work practices and demonstrating concern for health and well being of others.

The Vernon Company Safety Committee is dedicated to educating employees on safety awareness in order to prevent accidents before they happen; meet on a regular basis to discuss safety issues and suggest resolutions; to conduct safety audits of tools, equipment and the general working conditions; to remedy hazards that could potentially result in an injury or illness to our employees; to talk to and listen to other employees regarding safety and health – making sure appropriate issues are directed to the Safety Committee when necessary; to set forth safety rules and regulations as additional precautions; to remind employees of their safety responsibilities; and to influence attitudes toward safety and health in a proactive and positive manner.
