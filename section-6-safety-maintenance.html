Workplace Privacy

Purpose: To provide a safe and compliant workplace.

Policy: The Vernon Company recognizes the important of providing employees with a safe working environment and is responsible for the premises occupied by its employees and guests. On occasion, it may be necessary for management to inspect, search and access computer and phone information, offices, workstations, desks, lockers, or other storage areas for the purpose of ensuring compliance with company policies and state and federal laws, and for protection of employees and guests.

Workplace Violence

Purpose: The Vernon Company is committed to providing employees and guests with a safe, non-threatening work environment.

Policy: We are committed to providing a workplace where violence, threats of violence, intimidation and other disruptive behavior will not be tolerated. We will not allow weapons of any kind on Company property. Any who displays violent behavior, makes threats, exhibits threatening behavior, or brings a weapon of any kind on Company property, will be subject to removal from the property. If an investigation confirms that a violation of this Company policy has occurred, appropriate action will be taken, which may include arrest and prosecution, and/or discipline up to and including discharge (separation). To ensure that we maintain a safe, non-threatening work environment, it is imperative that employees report any conduct that violates this policy to their manager/supervisor or Human Resources, immediately.

Work-Related Accidents & Injuries/Illness

Purpose: To inform employees and to establish responsibility for reporting work-related accidents & injuries/illnesses.

Policy: The Vernon Company strives to provide safe working conditions for all employees. All employees can greatly assist in eliminating hazards by promptly reporting such conditions to your manager/supervisor or to Safety Management (or Human Resources). Programs will be implemented and maintained to provide reasonable assurance that The Vernon Company:

  • Complies with governmental and internal safety requirements.
  • Designs and operates its facilities to promote the safety and health of its employees and minimize safety and health risks arising from its operations.
  • Implements a training and accident process that informs employees about safety and protection measures.

If you are injured while on the job, please report it to your manager/supervisor and then to Human Resources, immediately. If your manager/supervisor is not available, report the injury directly to Human Resources. If the nature of your injury required the attention of a health care provider, you will be sent to a Company designated health care provider. Human Resources should schedule all workers’ compensation related appointments. A ‘Patient Status Report’ needs to be completed by the doctor and returned to Human Resources, following the appointment. Workers’ Compensation benefits apply to injuries or illnesses which are work-related, that is , arising out of and in the course of employment. When and if the health care provider assigns you restrictions or releases you to return to work, we require that you have the health care provider complete a ‘Patient Status Report’, and provide it to Human Resources on the day you receive it (before returning to work).

Safety Issues Reporting

Purpose: To maintain a safe work environment and to inform employees of the importance and procedure for reporting safety issues.

Policy: If an employee notices a safety issue related to our facilities, he/she should report it to their manager/supervisor or to a member of the Safety Committee for review and resolution.

Maintenance Issues Reporting

Purpose: To inform employees of the procedure for reporting maintenance issues.

Policy: If an employee notices that certain areas of a building, lights, water, faucets, kitchen area, restrooms, office equipment/furniture (or other related items) need general maintenance, they need to notify their manager/supervisor who should forward the issue to the Maintenance department by e-mail, or by calling ext. 8102. A work order will then be generated to inform maintenance employees to work on the issue by order of priority.

Parking

Purpose: To provide an adequate parking area for employees during work.

Policy: The Vernon Company has established a Parking policy to provide parking facilities and reduce congestion for employees on Company property. Human Resources will designate your parking spot on your first day of employment. If you have been in your designated parking spot for at least one (1) year, you can request a new spot with Human Resources.
Handicapped parking is provided for those who need it. A current handicapped parking permit is required. Please see Human Resources for this accommodation.

The speed limit in the parking areas is five (5) mph. This limit will be enforced for the safety of others. Parking courtesy and designated traffic flow patterns need to be observed.

Improper parking may result in removal of the car at the employee’s expense. The Vernon Company is not responsible for employee property in The Vernon Company parking lots.

Winter Parking

Purpose: To communicate changes in parking and caution employees when snow and ice arrives.

Policy; When winter weather is here and snow/ice falls, please keep in mind this procedure is in place to assist Maintenance with clearing snow from the parking lots in a timely manner.

If you come to work before 7:00 am (and the parking lot that you usually park in is not cleared of snow), please park in the far east parking lot (in the back, against the warehouse building). Do not park in the center of that lot if it not cleared of snow (please park along the building). You can move your car at your lunch, if needed.

Caution:

  • Please do not drive behind the truck which is plowing, as they may not see you.
  • Walk carefully on the snow and ice, use common sense and wear proper winter attire.
  • Remember, the parking lots and sidewalks will likely be slick
