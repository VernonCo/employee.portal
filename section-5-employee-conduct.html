Standards of Conduct

Purpose: To assure safe, efficient and harmonious operations and to inform all employees of
their responsibilities related to conduct at The Vernon Company.

Policy: The following is a partial list of behaviors, and actions which may result in disciplinary
measures, up to and including immediate discharge (separation of employment). Such
disciplinary action is at the sole discretion of management. The below list is not all inclusive,
and The Vernon Company reserves the right to discipline and/or discharge any employee at any
time. All of these behaviors include; while on duty (work time), on Company premises and/or
while representing the Company at a function.

  • Excessive absenteeism and tardiness.
  • Falsification of timecard.
  • Carrying a weapon.
  • Use of profane or abusive language.
  • Unauthorized leaving of the regularly assigned work location.
  • Fighting.
  • Insubordination.
  • Careless workmanship.
  • Misuse or sabotage of Company property.
  • Posting of unauthorized notices.
  • Falsification of records.
  • Removing, revealing, or otherwise misusing confidential information.
  • Unlawful use or possession of any drugs.
  • Use of alcohol (or coming to work while under the influence of alcohol).
  • Smoking in undesignated areas.
  • Restricting output.
  • Working in an unsafe manner and/or violation of Company Safety Rule.
  • Horseplay.
  • Poor housekeeping.
  • Loafing.
  • Theft.
  • Gambling.
  • Not demonstrating appropriate cleanliness and/or sanitation.
  • Sexual (or any other type of) harassment to an employee or anyone associated with The
    Vernon Company.
  • Non-professional appearance or performance.
  • Solicitation, distribution and/or access to Company premises by employees during nonworking hours.
  • Violation of a Company policy.
  • Unsatisfactory performance.
  • Any other misconduct.

Corrective Actions

Purpose: To inform employees of potential discipline steps.

Policy: The following are disciplinary actions in which The Vernon Company may take, when necessary. This is not an all-inclusive list of actions. The Company reserves the right to determine which corrective action is appropriate, based on the seriousness of breech of conduct. All counseling and/or warning discussions should be documented by the manager/supervisor.

These steps are available to management and may be followed as deemed appropriate, depending upon the nature of the infraction or the severity of the performance problem as well as the needs of The Vernon Company. Nothing contained in this policy is intended to change the ‘at-will’ status of the employee relationship or create an obligation to use these progressive steps in employee disciplines.

  • Disciplinary Counseling/Verbal Warning – will typically be given for minor violations.
  • Written Warning – will typically be given for repeated minor violations and for certain major violations. Repeated violation will lead to additional disciplinary action, up to and including suspension or discharge (separation of employment).
  • Probation or suspension – will occur in certain situations, depending on the severity and number of violations.
  • Discharge (Separation of Employment) – will occur in certain situations, depending on the severity and number of violations.

Anti-Harassment, Anti-Discrimination & Complaint Resolution Procedure

Purpose: To educate employees about harassment, discrimination and retaliation issues and provide a mechanism for employees to stop any such behavior from occurring.

Policy: The Vernon Company promotes a productive and safe work environment and does not tolerate any verbal or physical conduct by an employee which harasses, disrupts, or interferes with anther’s work performance or which create an intimidating, offensive or hostile environment, especially if such conduct concerns race, color, religion,sex, national origin, age, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, veteran status, or any other characteristic protected by law. It is the responsibility of all employees, whether managers, supervisors or co-workers, to maintain an environment free of harassment.

With respect to sexual harassment, the Company prohibits the following:

  • Unwelcome sexual advances; request for sexual favors; and all other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual or otherwise offensive nature, especially where:
    • Submission to such conduct is made either explicitly or implicitly a term or condition of employment;
    • Submission to or rejection of such conduct is used as the basis for decisions affecting an individual’s employment; or

Such conduct has the purpose or effect of creating an intimidating, hostile, or offensive working environment offensive comments, jokes, innuendoes, and other sexually-oriented statements or expressions. Examples of the type of conduct expressly prohibited by this policy include, but are not limited to the following:

  • Touching, such as rubbing or massaging someone’s neck or shoulders, stroking someone’s hair, or brushing against anther’s body.
  • Sexually suggestive touching.
  • Grabbing, groping, kissing, fondling.
  • Violating someone’s “personal space” in an intimidating or sexual manner.
  • Whistling or cat-calling.
  • Lewd, off-color, sexually oriented comments or jokes.
  • Foul or obscene language.
  • Leering, staring, stalking.
  • Suggestive or sexually explicit poster, calendars, photographs, graffiti, cartoon.
  • Unwanted or offensive letters, notes or poems.
  • Repeated requests for dates.
  • Offensive e-mail or voicemail messages.
  • Sexually oriented or explicit remarks, including written or oral references to sexual conduct, gossip regarding one’s sex life, body, sexual activities, deficiencies, or prowess.
  • Questions about one’s sex life or experiences.
  • Sexual favors in return for employment requests, or threats if sexual favors are not provided.
  • Sexual assault or rape.
  • Sitting or gesturing sexually.
  • Any other similar conduct or behavior deemed inappropriate by the Company.

With respect to discriminatory or harassing conduct based on a protected characteristic other than sex, any type of unwelcome conduct based on the protected characteristic, the submission to which is made a term or condition of the person’s employment, or the submission or rejection of which is made the basis of decisions affecting the person’s employment, or which has the purpose or effect of creating an abusive, offensive, intimidating, or hostile work environment is prohibited by this policy.

If an employee experience or witnesses any job-related harassment based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, veteran status, or any other characteristic protected by law, or believes that he or she has witnessed or been treated in an unlawful, discriminatory manner, he or she should promptly report the incident to a manger/supervisor, department/division head, or Human Resources.

This policy applies to all incidents of alleged harassment, including those which occur off-premises, or off-hours, where the alleged offender is a supervisor, co-worker, or even a non-employee, such as a vendor or customer, with whom the employee is involved, directly or indirectly, in a business or potential business relationship.

The Company takes complaints of discrimination an harassment very seriously. Thus, there is no need to follow any formal chain of command when filing a complaint or when discussing or expressing any issue of concern regarding alleged discrimination or harassment. Employees may bypass anyone is his or her direct chain of command and file a complaint, discuss, o r express any issue of concern with Human Resources at any time. The Company will undertake a prompt investigation of any complaints. Human Resources may be reached at: 641-792-9000.

If the Company determines that an employee has violated this policy, appropriate disciplinary action will be taken against the offending employee, up to and included possible termination of employment. If the Company determines a non-employee, such as a vendor or a customer, has violated this policy, appropriate action will be taken.

The Company prohibits any form of retaliation against any employee for filing a good faith complaint under this policy or assisting in a complaint investigation. Violation of this anti-retaliation will result in disciplinary action against the offending employee, up to and including possible termination of employment.

Attendance (including Bad Weather)

The Company expects all employees to be conscientious about attendance and punctuality at work. You are part of a team, and getting the work done depends on everyone being in the right place at the right time. Maintaining good attendance is a condition of employment and an essential function of your job.

Effective 10/01/2016, the PTO (Paid Time Off) days you accrue will replace all existing cation, birthday and floating holidays that you have been allotted under prior policies.

The purpose of Paid Time Off (PTO) is to provide employees with flexible paid time off from work that can be used for such needs a vacation, personal or family illness, doctor appointments, school, or other activities of the employee’s choice. Doctor’s notes no longer excuse illnesses so you will no longer need to provide a doctor’s note for routine sick days. Doctor’s notes still may be required for extended medical absences (A & S) or to comply with FMLA.

Excessive absenteeism for unapproved or unscheduled time off, as define by departmental need, will lead to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Other continuing pattern of absences, early departures, or tardiness – regardless of the exact number of days or hours — may also warrant disciplinary action. Any falsification of employee time cards, including incorrect reporting of time, will result in discipline, up to and including possinle termination of employment.

We do realize, however, that there are times when absences and tardiness cannot be avoided. Our no-fault system allows you to accumulate some Absentee Hours before any disciplinary action will be taken against you. When scheduling a full day of work, PTO must be used, if available. All other absences not designated as PTO will count towards your Absentee Hours and discipline will be administered as noted below in the Disciplinary Action section. Some Absentee Hours are expected; it is only when Absentee Hours become excessive, that an employee needs to be concerned about this or her attendance practices and job security.

Employee Proper Notification/Management Approval

If you know in advance you are going to be absent from work, you are required to notify your manager/supervisor in writing (e-mail is preferable; texting is NOT permissible) (at least one hour before your normal quit time, the business day prior to the absence. An example would be “give notice prior to 3:30 pm on Thursday if requesting all or portion of Friday off as vacation, or for a doctor’s appointment, etc”). The manger/supervisor will then determine if the time can be approved, based upon workload and based on the number of others already requested off within the department. Not everyone can be gone at the same time. You should not WAIT until the day before to request time off; make your request as soon as you know you want or need time off. Absences that are not approved will be dealt with more seriously and may be subject to discipline outside of the Attendance Policy (for example, someone whose time off request was not approved, but is absent anyway).

If you are ill or some other emergency prevents you from being at work on time or at all and did not allow for opportunity to request the leave the day before, you must telephone (texts are not permitted) your manager/supervisor as soon as possible.

If you do not give any notification and do not show up to work, we will consider it a No Call/No Show and your total hours missed from work will be added to your Absentee Hours. Any employee who fails to report to work without notification to his or her manger/supervisor for a period of three consecutive work days or more will be considered to have voluntarily terminated his or her employment relationship.

All absences will count toward your Absentee Hours with the following Exceptions

  • PTO (previously referred to as Vacation, Birthday, & Float Holidays)
  • Non-disciplinary Leaves
    • FMLA, A & S, Workers’ Compensation, Jury Duty or Subpoenaed as a Witness, Military Duty, Bereavement, and any other government mandated leaves.
  • No office-work available, to be determined/requested by management.
  • A DOT issued ‘no-travel warning’ or tornado warning siren, or Newton schools (or your local school) closing because of weather conditions.

Attendance Form for Manger/Supervisor to Use

After an employee has notified a manger/supervisor of a need for leave, the manager/supervisor of a need for leave, the manage/supervisor must report the absence under this this policy using the Attendance Form found on our computer network (in Excel). The form, along with the employee’s written request, must be forwarded to Human Resources prior to the next pay day.

Disciplinary Action

Unscheduled or unapproved absences from work are considered to be a work-performance problem, which need correction. To that end, everyone has a right to know when repeated absences have reached a point that they are, or could lead to disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment. Therefore, the Company will generally treat unpaid Absentee Hours in the following manner:

Step 1: 24 hours total in previous rolling 12 month period = verbal warning.
Step 2: 40 hours total in previous rolling 12 month period = written warning.
Step 3: 56 hours total in previous rolling 12 month period = written warning + 1 day suspension.

  • The day is suspension will be the Company’s discretion.
  • Disciplinary suspension hours will be designated as PTO. If PTO is not available, the suspension hours will be unpaid and added to Absentee Hours.

Step 4: Any future lates/absences in subsequent 12 month period will result in immediate termination.

The same procedure will be followed for active Regular part-time administrative employees with Absentee Hours pro-rated based on their scheduled hours worked per day.

Exempt Employees

Please note that exempt employees’ work hours may vary from department to department. In addition, exempt employees are expected to work at least forty (40) hours in a work week and typically work more after hours, without overtime pay. Exempt employees will be monitored by their management for attendance issues and dealt with on an individual basis, according to this policy.

Bad Weather

If there are not DOT warnings or school delays/closings and you are still not comfortable with winter weather conditions and do not want to risk driving, you always have the option of coming in late or staying home.

We recognize that employees may have difficulty reporting to work at times due to ice/snow. We will follow the DOT and school system announcements regarding delays and school closings (in Newton, or your local school system) for employees to avoid hours to their Absentee Hours.

Please be sure to call into the office as you would with any other late or absence. Any hours missed from work will be added to your PTO or Absentee Hours. Please use good judgement and discretion.

Conflict of Interest

Purpose: To communicate The Vernon Company’s Conflict of Interest policy.

Policy: A conflict of interest is defined as any situation in which an employee could appear to benefit, at the expense of the Company. Employees should not engage in any business activity that conflicts with, or is contrary to, the interests of The Vernon Company. It is expected that ethical standards will prevent any Vernon employee from providing services or assistance which otherwise could be provided by the Company.

All exempt employees, and those employees who are involved with purchasing, are required to sign a Conflict of Interest Statement on an annual basis. The Corporate Secretary will retain these documents for future reference.

Employees are responsible for reporting any situation they perceive to be a conflict of interest, or any potential conflict of interest, to their manager/supervisor and Human Resources.

Employment & Supervision

Purpose: To provide a fair work environment and to decrease to eliminate issues of conflict, favoritism and employee morale problems as they relate to reporting relationships.

Policy: It is The Vernon Company’s policy that relatives of current employees of the Company in a salaried (exempt) or administrative (nonexempt) position may be hired as an employee only if they are will not directly report to or supervise a relative. If already employed, the employee cannot be transferred into such a reporting relationship. If the relative relationship is established after employment begins, the Executive Committee of The Vernon Company will decide who will be transferred where. If that option is not available, one of the individuals will be required to resign.

It is well accepted that employment of relatives in the same area of an organization can caused serious conflicts with regard of favoritism and employee morale. In these circumstances, all parties, including manager/supervisors, leave themselves open to inequitable issues such as work assignments, transfer opportunities, performance evaluations, promotions, demotions, disciplinary actions, and/or terminations (separations). In addition environment may be carried to day-to-day working relationships.

In regard to this policy, a relative is defined as: current legal spouse (including common-lay), parent, step-parent, child, step-child, brother (including in-lay), sister (including in-lay), and step-sibling. This policy also applies to individuals who are not legally related but who reside with another employee in a ‘significant-other’ relationship (the phrase ‘domestic partner’ has been used to describe persons who are also in this type of relationship).

The Executive Committee of The Vernon Company must approve any exception to this policy.

Genetic Information Nondiscrimination

The Company complies with Title II of the Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act of 2008 (GINA), which protects applicants and employees from discrimination based on genetic information in hiring, promotion, termination, pay, fringe benefits, and all other terms and conditions of employment. “Genetic information” includes information about genetic tests of applicants, employees or their family members; the manifestation of diseases and disorders in family members (family medical history); and requests for or receipt of genetic services by applicants, employees, or their family members.

In accordance with GINA, the Company does not intentionally collect, maintain or use genetic information about applicants to employees their family members in any decision concerning whether to hire an applicant or the terms, conditions or any aspect of employment of an employee. GINA permits certain limited circumstances under which an employer may acquire genetic information, such as:

  • inadvertently obtaining the information;
  • requiring family medical history in compliance with Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) certification provisions; or
  • acquiring the information through publicly available documents containing family medical history information.

Any genetic information collected in connection with the approved circumstances outlined above will maintained in the same type of separate confidential files required for medical information under the Americans with Disabilities Act. Genetic information may not be disclosed without employee consent exempt in limited circumstances.

If you believe or suspect the Company has acquired, disclosed or used genetic information in violation of GINA, or has otherwise discriminated against any applicant or employee on the basis of genetic information, promptly report the suspected violation to your supervisor, department or division manager, or the Human Resources Department. You may bypass anyone in your direct chain of command and file your complaint or discuss or express any issue of concern with the Human Resources Department. You may bypass anyone in your direct chain of command and file your complaint or discuss or express any issue of concern with the Human Resources Department at any time.

The Company prohibits any form of retaliation against any employee for filing a complaint under this policy or for assisting or participating in a complaint investigation. Violation of this anti-retaliation policy will result in disciplinary action against the offending employee, up to and including possible termination of employment.

Tobacco Free Workplace

Purpose: The Vernon Company is committed to providing a safe, healthy, comfortable and productive environment for all employees, contractors, visitors, and guests of The Vernon Company.

Policy: Effective November 1st, 2011, all Vernon owned building structures and property became entirely tobacco-free. Smoking or other use of tobacco or tobacco-like products during business hours, or on Vernon campus, at any time, is strictly prohibited.

Prohibition includes throughout all Vernon facilities and property and when traveling on Company business. This policy applies to all employees, contractors, visitors or guest on Vernon property.

The use of tobacco is prohibited:

  • In all areas within Vernon Company buildings
  • On all property maintained by Vernon Company, whether leased or owned, adjacent sidewalks and parking lots
  • In all vehicles owned, leased or rented by The Vernon Company
  • In all employee vehicles when parked on Vernon Company property

Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary action.

Drug Use & Testing

Purpose and Policy:

  • Purpose of The Vernon Company’s Drug Use and Testing Policy
    The Company recognizes that drug and alcohol use and abuse in the workplace constitutes an obstacle to the profitable, efficient, and safe operation of its business. By enacting a drug testing policy, the Company hopes to combat the problems associated with substance abuse by creating a drug free workplace. In doing so, the Company has established the following drug testing policy.
  • Definitions
    • “Alcohol” means ethanol, isopropanol, or methanol
    • “Drug” means a substance considered a controlled substance and included in schedule I, II, III, IV or V under the Federal Controlled Substance Act, 21 U.S.C. § 801 et seq.
    • “Employee” means a person in the service of an employer in this state, and manager/supervisor, or officer of the employer who is actively involved in the day-to-day operations of the business.
    • “Medical review officer” means a licensed physician assistant authorized to practice in any state of the United States, who is responsible for receiving laboratory results generated by an employer’s drug or alcohol testing program, and who has knowledge of substance abuse disorders and has appropriate medical training to interpret and evaluate in individual’s confirmed positive test result together with the individual’s medical history and any other relevant biomedical information.
    • “Prospective employee” means a person who has made application, whether written or oral, to an employer to become an employee.
    • “Legal drug” means a drug for which the individual has a prescription, or an over-the counter drug.
  • The Company Work Rules Involving the Use of Drugs
    • Use of Prescription Drugs: An employee may take a prescription drug during work hours only if the drug has been prescribed for the employee by a physician or another authorized prescriber and only if the drug is taken according to the directions o the prescription and its use will not impair the employee’s ability to do his/her assigned essential duties in a safe and effective manner.
    • Use of Over-the-Counter Drugs: An employee may take an over-the-counter during during work hours only if the drug is used for its intended purposes and it is taken according to the medication’s instructions, or in it is prescribed for the employee by a physician or another authorized prescriber, the drug is taken according to the directions of the employee’s physician or other authorized prescriber and it use will not impair the employee’s ability to do his/her assigned essential duties in a safe and effective manner.
    • Abuse of Legal Drugs: Abuse of legal drugs, as noted in (1) and (2) above, will not be tolerated and the Company will deal with such abuse in a the same manner as it does the use of controlled substances.
    • Notice to Safety Coordinator: An employee must notify the Company’s Safety Directory whenever he or she is using either a prescription drug or an over-the-counter drug if the use of such drug may potentially adversely affect the individual’s ability to safely perform his/her essential duties at work. Upon such notification, Company reserves the right to take appropriate steps to protect the safety of its employees, including the right to relive the employee f his/her assigned duties during the period of usage.
    • Drug or Alcohol Possession or Use: Except as delineated in paragraph (1) or (2) above, an employee who possesses, uses, consumes, sells, transfer, or attempts to sell or transfer any prescription drug, any controlled substance or alcoholic beverage while on Company premises, or while on duty, whether or not that individual is on Company premises, is guilty of misconduct and is subject to discipline, including suspension without pay or termination, even for a first offense and without the necessity of a drug [or alcohol] test.
    • Impairment During Work Hours: An employee who appears to be impaired during work hours due to the effects of (1) the use of alcohol, (2) the abuse of a legal drug, or (3) the use of a controlled substance will not be allowed to work and is subject to discipline, including suspension without pay or termination, even for a first offense and without the necessity of a drug or alcohol test.
    • Impairment Outside of Working Time: If it is determined that an employee is impaired while operating a company vehicle, but not during work hours, due to the affects of the use of alcohol, any legal drug or a controlled substance, that individual will be subject to discipline, including suspension without pay or termination, even for a first offense and without the necessity of a drug or alcohol test. Prior to a determination of the discipline, to be imposed, the employee will be required to meet with a senior company officer. In addition, the discipline imposed for alcohol or legal drug use may be subject to modification, in the sole discretion of management, depending upon any recommendation by The Vernon Company’s insurance provider.
  • Pre-Employment Testing
    • Drug Testing: The Company will conduct pre-employment drug testing that is designed to prevent hiring individuals who use controlled substances or who abuse legal drugs. This policy covers applicants for positions that are not covered by federal drug testing regulations.
      • The following is a list of drugs for which testing s conducted:
        • Marijuana (including cannabinoids and THC derivatives)
        • Cocaine
        • Amphetamines (including methamphetamines)
        • PCP
        • Opiates (including heroin, codeine, morphine)
      • At the time of the collection of the sample, the prospective employee may provide to the Medical Review Officer (MRO) any information that may be relevant to the drug test. Such information may include identification of prescription or non-prescription currently or recently used or any other relevant medical information. To the extent feasible, precautions will be taken to insure that the testing only measures and the records concerning the testing only make use of, the information regarding use of drugs in the body.
    • Prospective Employee in Connection with a Drug Test: A prospective employee’s confirmed positive drug test result, or the refusal of the prospective employee to provide a testing sample, will result in the Company’s refusal to hire the prospective employee for twelve (12) months. This result does not prevent the prospective employee from later initiating another inquiry with the Company. The applicant must receive a valid “negative” report on the drug screen to be hired. If that qualification is not met, the prospective employee will be deemed “not qualified,” and either the application process will be terminated or any conditional offer of employment that has been extended will be withdrawn. A confirmed positive test also constitutes misconduct sufficient to disqualify the test subject from receiving unemployment compensation benefits. At the time the specimen is collected for drug testing, it will be split into two components. If there is a “positive” test result reported to the Company by the MRO for the first test, the applicant will be notified in writing of the result through a letter, sent certified mail, return receipt requested. The cost of the first test is paid for by the Company. The applicant may have a second test. performed on the “second half” of the split sample or specimen, at the applicant’s own cost, at an “approved” laboratory of the applicant’s own selection. If an applicant desires to have that second, confirmatory test done, the Company must receive notification within seven (7) days of the date of the mailing of the letter to the applicant – including both receipt of the payment and designation of the laboratory. If the second test is conducted and the applicant receives a negative result, the Company shall pay for the second test.
  • Costs of Tests
    • The Company pays the actual costs of the testing (see D.2 for details).
  • Drug Awareness/Drug-Free Workplace
    • Please see the Company’s Drug Free Workplace policy contained in this manual.

The Company reserves the right to review the policies and procedures contained in the Drug Use and Testing Policy, and reserves the right to change, revise or terminate the policy at any time. Nothing in this policy is intended nor should be construed as requiring “for cause” termination or otherwise affecting the at-will nature of the employment relationship.

Jewelry, Tattoos, Unnatural Hair Color

The Company recognizes that personal appearance is n important element of self-expression and strives not to control or dictate appropriate employee appearance, specifically with regards to jewelry, tattoos, or unnatural hair color worn as a matter of personal choice.

In keeping with this approach, The Vernon Company allows reasonable self-expression through personal appearance, unless it poses a conflict with the following:

  • Employee’s ability to perform his or her position effectively within his or her specific work environment.
  • Offensive or harassing toward co-workers or vendors with whom The Vernon Company conducts business.
  • Corporate or societal norms

If management determines an employee’s jewelry, unnatural hair color or tattoos may present such a conflict, the employee will be encouraged to identify appropriate options. Such options may include (but not strictly limited to) removal of excess or offensive jewelry, covering of tattoos, changing unnatural hair color, transfer to an alternative position, or other reasonable means to resolve the conflict.

Conclusion: If clothing fails to meet these standards, as determined by employee’s manager or HR staff, the employee will be asked not to wear the inappropriate item to work again.

If the problem persists, the employee may be sent home to change clothes and will receive a verbal warning for the first offense. All other policies about personal time use will apply. Progressive disciplinary action will be applied if dress code violations continue.

Generative Artificial Intelligence (AI) Chatbot Usage

Purpose: With the increasing popularity of generative AI chatbots such as OpenAI’s ChatGPT and Google’s Bard, it has become necessary to outline the proper use of such tools while working at The Vernon Company. While we remain committed to adopting new technologies to aid our mission when possible, we also understand the risks and limitations of generative AI chatbots and want to ensure responsible use. Our goal is to protect employees, account executives, clients, suppliers, customers, and the Company from harm.

Overview: While AI chatbots can be used to perform a variety of functions, this policy addresses only the use of a web-based interface to ask or “prompt” the chatbot in a conversational manner to find answers to questions or to create or edit written content.

Some examples of what could be created using an AI chatbot include:

  • Emails and letters.
  • Blog posts, reports and other publications.
  • Sales and advertising copy.
  • Policies and job descriptions.
  • Spreadsheet calculations.
  • Foreign language translations.
  • Coding development or debugging.
  • Document or information sorting.
  • Outlines or summaries of internal or external information.

There are, however, risks in using this technology, including uncertainty about who owns the AI-created content and security/privacy concerns with inputting proprietary Company information or sensitive information about an employee, account executive, client, customer, etc., when interacting with the chatbot. Additionally, the accuracy of the content created by these technologies cannot be relied upon, as the information may be outdated, misleading or—in some cases—fabricated.

Eligibility: This policy applies to all employees of The Vernon Company and to all work associated with The Vernon Company that those employees perform, whether on or off Company premises.

Policy: Limited use of generative AI chatbots will be allowed while performing work for The Vernon Company with the approval of your Manager. Company email addresses, credentials or phone numbers can be used to create an account with these technologies. No Company data of any kind may be submitted (copied, typed, etc.) into these platforms.

All AI-generated content must be carefully reviewed for accuracy, valid business sense and verified as truthful before relying on it for work purposes. If a reliable source cannot be found to verify factual information generated by the chatbot, that information cannot be used for work purposes. The content must also not infringe on any company’s trademarks, copyrights, patents of proprietary information.

Acceptable uses include:

  • For general-knowledge questions meant to enhance your understanding on a work-related topic.
  • To brainstorm ideas related to projects you are working on.
  • To create formulas for Excel spreadsheets or similar programs.
  • To develop or debug code, to be verified before deployment.
  • To draft an email or letter.
  • To generate subject lines.
  • To summarize online research or to create outlines for content projects to assist in full coverage of a topic.
  • To research types of leads for account executives.

Unacceptable uses include:

  • Copying and pasting, typing, or in any way submitting Company content or data of any kind into the AI chatbot.

Any violation of this policy will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

Ethical Use: Employees must use generative AI chatbots in accordance with all The Vernon Company’s conduct and antidiscrimination policies. These technologies must not be used to create content that is inappropriate, discriminatory, or otherwise harmful to others or the Company. Such use will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

Monitoring: The Vernon Company’s Computer Use Policy still applies, and Company reserves the right to monitor and track usage when using generative AI chatbots with Company equipment or on Company networks.

If you have any questions regarding this policy, please contact Human Resources.

Cellular Phone Use

Purpose: To provide guidelines for use of personal cellular phones during working hours.

Policy: The following rules apply to cellular phones owned by employees. Individuals who violate these rules will be subject to discipline, up to and including termination.

Personal Use

It is recognized that from time to time employees may need to place or receive personal phone calls during working hours. Cellular phones may be used on a limited basis with prior management approval for the situation. However, the number and duration of these calls should be kept to a minimum and should, when possible, occur during breaks or non-working hours. Employees should use discretion and good judgement when making personal calls. (with prior approval) so as not to disturb others. No personal calls are permitted in situations where they would disrupt any business operations. Cellular phones should be turned off(or the ringer silenced), especially in meetings or other situations where noise would be disruptive.
