Workweek

Purpose: To communicate typical work days and hours.

Policy: The Vernon Company (office) normal workweek is Monday through Friday. Typically, each work day for a full-time employee consists of either (8) hours, and normal work hours are 7:30am to 4:30pm. However, starting times may vary according to the season of the year and position requirements. Alterations of seasonal schedules may be necessary to accommodate customers. Your manger/supervisor will advise you of necessary changes regarding work hours, if and when needed. In addition, employees are provided with some flexibility in hours for situations that may arise (to be coordinated with their manger/supervisor).

Time card Administration

Purpose: To communicate the process for administrative (nonexempt) employees to submit their work time for pay.

Policy/Procedure: The Federal Wage & Hour Law requires the Company to keep a record of the time each administrative (nonexempt) employee works. To accomplish this, employees must write in and out times at the beginning and end of each work period on time cards provided. After the last entry of the workweek, sign and give your time card to your manger/supervisor for his/her signature. Time cards are required to be signed by both the employee and supervisor/manager before turning in to Payroll. When an administrative (nonexempt) employee does not work eight (8) hours in a day (or their scheduled hours in a day), the manager/supervisor must fill-out an Attendance Form (which can be printed from Excel. This form needs to be attached to the employee’s time card and given to Payroll (also, attach an e-mail with the request for time off from the employee). If you have questions concerning your time card, please contact your manager/supervisor.

A time card is a legal document. Time cards must accurately reflect all time worked. Misrepresentation of time worked or forgery is a violation of the law. Falsification of Company records to include intentional incorrect reporting of time will result in discipline, up to and including termination of employment. An audit of time cards may be conducted at any time at the discretion of the Company, as needed, utilizing the Brivo Access Control Security System.

Overtime Pay

Purpose: To communicate the process for full-time administrative (nonexempt) employees to receive overtime pay.

Policy: Overtime pay will be paid at one and one-half (1.5) times the normal hourly pay rate for those employees who are eligible. All overtime work should be approved in advance by the manager/supervisor of the employee. The Vernon Company will provide overtime pay to full-time administrative (nonexempt) employees as follows:

  • All hours worked in excess of forty (40) hours per week (see exception)
  • Exception: during weeks when the Company is closed to observe a Company paid holiday, hours not worked due to the Company paid holiday will be added to employees’ total worked hours in that week.
  • Example: During the week of Memorial Day, the Company is closed on Monday due to the holiday. If employee works 35 hours during the remaining 4 days of that week, employee would receive credit for working 43 hours during week and get paid 8 hours holiday pay, 32 hours regular pay, plus 3 hours overtime pay.)
  • Hours worked on Company paid holidays (Note: See Note before for Company holidays that will be paid at this overtime rate.)

Note: Company paid holiday are listed in the ‘Holidays‘ section of this handbook.

Pay Periods

Purpose: To communicate pay period details and paycheck distribution schedules.

Policy: Administrative (nonexempt) employees will be paid each Friday for the workweek, ending the previous Sunday. Salaried (exempt) employees will be paid on the last Friday of each month. Note that holidays may slightly change the normal payment schedules.

Payment will be made by direct deposit to two (2) or more banks of your choice, as long as the bank accepts ACH transactions. At no time will pay be advanced to employees prior to hours worked. Paychecks/advances will be distributed by Human Resources. If an employee’s paycheck is not received personally, a receipt must be signed by the person receiving the check for that employee.

Wage & Salary Administration

Purpose: To communicate proper pay practices and make good faith efforts to ensure that deductions are in accordance with the law.

Policy: Your earnings are based on the administration of wages and salary according to applicable laws and the Employment Status Definitions section of this handbook.

Reducing Pay for Hours Not Worked (Docking)

Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) – The FLSA establishes minimum wage, overtime pay, equal pay, record-keeping, and child labor standards for employees who are covered by the act. Pursuant to the FLSA, nonexempt employees are entitled to a mandated minimum wage, as well as overtime pay of not less than one and one half (1.5) times their regular hourly rate of pay for all hours worked in excess of forty (40) in their workweek.

If you are nonexempt, your pay may be reduced (docked) for such reason as tardiness, personal time off, early departure, disciplinary suspension, or absences without sufficient notification or justification.

If you are exempt, your pay may be reduced (docked) for:

  • Absences from work for one or more full days for personal reasons, other than sickness or disability. Less than full day absences for these reasons do not constitute a deduction.
  • Penalties imposed in good faith for infractions of safety rules of major significance.
  • Unpaid disciplinary suspensions of one or more full days imposed in good faith for infractions of workplace conduct rules, and imposed pursuant to a written policy applicable to all employees.
  • Absences during the initial or last week of employment, in which the exempt employee does not work the full week.
  • Absences (partial or full day) that constitute unpaid leave under the Family and Medical Leave Act (for personal or eligible relatives).
  • Offsets of military or witness or jury pay received by an employee for a particular week for absences occasioned by military leave or jury or witness duty.

Improper Docking Complaints

The Vernon Company is committed to proper pay practices and policies. If an employee’s pay is found by the Company to have been incorrectly docked, the Company will reimburse the employee for any improper deductions and will make a good faith effort to ensure that any future deductions are in accordance with the law. Employees who believe that their pay has been improperly docked may discuss the complaint with Human Resources.
